CASE FILE 03 · MOVEMENT PATTERN

Location metadata

Riley is now your prime suspect.

But there’s a challenge: she’s on a mission, location classified.

Official records don’t reveal her address — yet you start digging. You decide once again to use metadata to track her location

Phones, fitness apps (for example Strava), maps, and wearables record coordinates or cell sectors. Even coarse points repeated over time reveal home (night repeats), work (weekday daytime repeats), and sensitive stops (rare places that matter).

Real-world parallels

  • Strava heatmaps have exposed patrol routes and base layouts when military personnel shared workout data—a metadata leak without private messages. See more
  • Aggregated location is often enough to deanonymize individuals in datasets that were “anonymous.” See more
